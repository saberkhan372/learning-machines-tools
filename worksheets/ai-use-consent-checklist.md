# AI Use + Consent Checklist

Use this before, during, and after the camp to keep participation visible, optional, and discussable.

## Participation Choice

Choose any pathway for any activity.

| Pathway | What It Means |
| --- | --- |
| Use | Try the AI tool directly and document what happens. |
| Observe / Critique | Analyze pre-generated examples without logging into a tool. |
| Teach / Design | Create a lesson, worksheet, or facilitation plan. |
| Build / Code | Make an explainer or small interactive tool. |
| Critical / No-AI | Create a critique, consent checklist, model card, policy, or unplugged activity. |

## Camp Norms

- Human learning comes first.
- AI use is optional, visible, and discussable.
- No one has to use a tool they object to.
- AI outputs are not neutral.
- We name labor, consent, data, and bias concerns.
- We avoid casual imitation of living artists by name.
- We do not generate or simulate real people without consent.
- We review public summaries carefully.
- We preserve room for uncertainty, refusal, and disagreement.

## Consent Questions

- How would you like your name to appear in public recaps?
- May your comments be quoted or summarized?
- May your project be mentioned?
- May your project be linked?
- May screenshots or clips of your work appear in recaps or recordings?
- Do you have concerns about recordings, transcripts, AI-generated recaps, or public summaries?

## Required Acknowledgment

This camp includes critical investigation of generative AI tools and systems. Some activities may involve using AI tools directly, while others offer non-AI, observation, critique, or classroom-design pathways. We will discuss data, consent, labor, bias, ownership, attribution, privacy, and creative practice.

Choose one:

- I understand and am comfortable participating.
- I understand and would prefer low-AI or non-AI options.
- I have concerns and would like more information.

## Recap and Attribution Protocol

- Use the display name participants provide.
- Ask before quoting comments, linking projects, or sharing screenshots.
- Review AI-assisted summaries manually before publication.
- Provide a correction path for names, pronouns, project links, and attribution.
- Do not treat silence as consent for public sharing.

## Reflection

- What did the human decide?
- What did the machine generate, predict, classify, complete, or distort?
- What data, defaults, interface choices, or platform assumptions shaped the result?
- Who might be copied, misrepresented, erased, stereotyped, or excluded?
- What would responsible use require?
- What would opting out look like?
