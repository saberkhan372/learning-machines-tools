# Welcome Video Script

Status: **ready to record**
Date: 2026-07-10 (day before Session 1)
Runtime: ~425 words ≈ 2:55 at a measured teaching pace (145 wpm); 2:40 if
you read slightly quicker.
Companion to the [Participant Welcome](../pages/participant-welcome.html)
page — this is the spoken version of the same essentials, plus the
Calendar/Classroom activation asks from the July 10 invite-tracker review.

Merged from two drafts (Claude + Codex): the schedule, positioning edge,
and consent commitments from one; the Calendar/Classroom asks,
guiding-questions preview, and "come as you are" close from the other.

---

## Script

**[0:00 — talking head, plain background]**

Hi, I'm Saber. I run CC Fest, and I'm your host for Learning Machines.
Welcome. I'm glad you're here.

Here's what this camp is. Three Saturday sessions on Zoom: July 11, 18,
and 25, from 9 to 11 in the morning, Pacific time. One session on how
machines write. One on how they imagine images. One on how they make
video.

**[0:25 — the frame; optional cut to site homepage]**

And here's what it is not. It is not a prompt-engineering webinar, and it
is not a sales pitch. The question we're asking is simpler and harder:
what is the machine actually doing? Each week we take a generative system
apart, run small experiments, and ask: what is the model predicting? What
does it assume when we leave things unsaid? Whose data, labor, and
creative work is inside the output? You'll leave with claims you can back
up.

**[0:55 — who's in the room]**

You're joining educators, artists and designers, technologists, students,
and curious people from four continents. Some of you use these tools
every day. Some of you have real reservations. Some of you have decided
not to use them at all. Every one of those positions is welcome, and none
of them will be argued away. Every activity has a path that never
requires you to touch an AI tool.

**[1:20 — what you need; optional cut to a tool in the browser]**

What do you need to prepare? Nothing. No coding background, no accounts,
no installs, no paid tools. A browser and the Zoom chat box are the whole
kit. One boundary so nobody's surprised: we investigate how models
behave. We don't train them from scratch.

**[1:45 — logistics + the ask; optional cut to Classroom, then Start Here]**

Everything lives in two places. Google Classroom is our shared home:
materials, recordings, recaps. The website has the tools and a Start Here
guide. Every session is recorded, and recordings go to registered
participants only. If 9 a.m. Pacific is the middle of your night, and for
some of you it is, the async route is a full path through this camp, not
a consolation prize. Two small asks: if you haven't joined Classroom yet,
please do. And if you plan to attend live, respond to the Calendar invite
when you can.

**[2:25 — consent + close; back to talking head]**

Cameras are optional. You choose how you're named in anything we share.
And our recordings are never used to train AI models.

Before Saturday, take five minutes in Classroom and Start Here. Then come
as you are, and bring one honest question about these machines. We'll
spend three Saturdays finding out how much of it we can answer together.
See you there.

## Delivery notes

- **Pace.** The beats land at ~2:55 at a normal teaching pace. Don't rush
  the "what it is not" paragraph: the investigation positioning is what
  converted this cohort (investigation-goal respondents accepted at the
  highest rate), so let it sit.
- **The one line to keep verbatim:** *"We investigate how models behave.
  We don't train them from scratch."* It's already on Start Here, and
  train-your-own-model questions kept arriving anyway — the video is the
  best remaining chance to land it.
- **The asks are deliberately soft.** "If you plan to attend live" keeps
  the Calendar ask optional: the invite tracker showed Classroom-only
  people are largely the async lane working as designed, not drop-off.
  Don't harden it into an RSVP push.
- **The async line names the hard case on purpose** ("the middle of your
  night") — the IST and PHT registrants should hear themselves
  acknowledged, not euphemized.
- **Screen cues are optional.** It works as a straight talking-head. If
  you cut to screens, the Diffusion Step-Through Viewer mid-drag is the
  most legible five seconds of "what this camp feels like."
- **Consent triple is a commitment, not boilerplate** — cameras optional,
  naming choice, never used to train models — it restates the Consent and
  Recap Protocol on camera.
