## SLIDE 1 — Title

**Session 4: Showcase**
*What did we learn about generative AI by making with it?*

Learning Machines: Text, Images, Video — CC Fest

---

## SLIDE 2 — Opening Frame

> This is the session where the camp becomes yours.

Over three sessions we asked the same question in three registers:

- **Session 1:** What is a language model doing when it writes?
- **Session 2:** What is an image model doing when it generates?
- **Session 3:** What changes when generation has to work across time?

**Today you show what you found.**

*Speaker note: Keep this brief. The session belongs to participants, not to the facilitator.*

---

## SLIDE 3 — Norms for the Share-Out

- Respond to **evidence**, not to impressiveness
- "That surprised me" is a complete response
- "I don't know what to make of that" is a complete response
- We name what the **human decided**
- The Critical / No-AI pathway is equal to every other pathway

| Time | Block |

---

## SLIDE 5 — Presentation Format

**8–10 minutes per participant.**

After each presentation, the group asks two questions only:

1. **"What did you notice?"** — factual observation, not evaluation
2. **"What question does this open?"** — something the project raised

*No praise. No general feedback. These two questions keep responses substantive.*

*Speaker note: The two-question format is critical. Open feedback drifts into "this was great" or "have you thought about X?" — neither is useful. Evidence-based observation and genuine questions are.*

## SLIDE 6 — Pathway: Make

**A creative artifact — text, image, or video — with process evidence.**

Show:
- At least three versions
- What the model did vs. what you decided
- What you revised, rejected, or kept

The process documentation is part of the project.

## SLIDE 7 — Pathway: Teach

**A classroom-ready activity, worksheet, or facilitation plan.**

Show:
- The activity or worksheet
- A 2-minute version of the activity with the group (if possible)
- One low-AI / no-AI pathway

Teaching an AI investigation is itself an investigation.

---

## SLIDE 8 — Pathway: Investigate

**A model behavior experiment or Default Test.**

Show:
- Your prompt(s) and at least three examples or variations
- Your written evidence-based claim
- One thing that surprised you and one thing that confirmed what you expected

---

## SLIDE 9 — Pathway: Explain

**A tool, poster, zine, or concept bridge.**

Show:
- The artifact
- Walk through the core idea in 2 minutes
- What mechanism you were trying to make visible

---

## SLIDE 10 — Pathway: Critical / No-AI

**A critique, consent checklist, model card, policy draft, or unplugged activity.**

This pathway is not a consolation. Some of the most important work in this field is critical.

Show:
- The artifact
- What claim or argument it makes
- What evidence it draws on

---

## SLIDE 11 — If You Didn't Finish

*"I didn't finish"* is not a reason not to share.

Show what you have.

What you found in the process is the project.

*Speaker note: Say this out loud before presentations start. Unfinished work often produces the most interesting conversations.*

---

## SLIDE 12 — COLLECTIVE REFLECTION

**Round 1 — One thing you are taking back:**

Go around. Each person completes:

> *"I am taking back ___."*

No elaboration. Facilitator records these.

---

## SLIDE 13 — Round 2: One Question You Are Still Holding

> *"I am still wondering ___."*

These questions become the shared open archive of the cohort.

*Speaker note: Write these down. They become the input for future camp iterations and for second-wave tool decisions.*

---

## SLIDE 14 — Round 3: What the Machine Can't Do

Open discussion — 10 minutes.

> *"Based on what you saw: what is something the machine genuinely cannot do that you think matters?"*

Push for specificity:
- Not *"understand"* — but what would understanding require?
- Not *"be creative"* — but what in the creative process was outside the model's contribution?

*Speaker note: You don't need to resolve this. The goal is a more precise vocabulary for talking about AI limits.*

---

## SLIDE 15 — CLOSING

> What we did this camp was investigate. Not evaluate. Not celebrate. Not panic. Investigate.

You built a protocol:

> **Hypothesis → controlled test → output comparison → evidence-based claim → ethical reflection**

That protocol is portable. It works on any model, any modality, any tool.

The repeated question — **what is the machine actually doing?** — does not have a final answer. The systems change. But the practice of asking, with evidence, with specificity, with attention to whose labor and whose images and whose stories shaped the training data — that practice is yours now.
