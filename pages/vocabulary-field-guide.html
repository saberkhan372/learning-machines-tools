Visual Examples

learning machines

Token

Text gets broken into chunks the model can count and predict. The chunks may not match words. Try it in the Tokenizer.

Temperature

A low setting favors the most likely next token. A higher setting lets less-likely continuations enter the sample. Watch the shift in the Tokenizer.

Default

When a prompt is vague, the system fills in bodies, settings, styles, and roles. Compare those choices in the Default Test.

Diffusion

The image is refined step by step from noisy structure toward a prompt-guided result. Use the Diffusion Viewer to pause each stage.

Drift

The subject keeps changing when it should stay stable. Temporal tools make drift visible frame by frame, especially Temporal Telephone.

Coherence

Frames belong together: identity, objects, camera, and physics remain consistent. Inspect that with the Frame-by-Frame Viewer.

Hallucination

A response can sound fluent and specific while lacking grounding. Treat it as a claim to verify in the Confidence Is Not Truth Explorer.

Human-in-the-loop

Human judgment belongs before, during, and after generation: setting purpose, checking evidence, naming limits, and deciding what to do next. Document that with the Model Card.
