Machines that imagine

Images

When an image model fills in a prompt, what does it assume?

Pixels, features, diffusion, and defaults. We start by predicting what a vague prompt might draw, then inspect how images become grids, regions, noise, and evidence.

A.

What we make visible

01

Pixels

Images become number grids before they become anything a model can process.

02

Features

Edges, regions, color, and texture become signals that support recognition.

03

Diffusion

Generation starts from noise and iteratively moves toward prompt-consistent structure.

04

Defaults

When prompts are vague, assumptions about bodies, settings, and style get drawn.

B.

Critical / No-AI

D.

Run of show · 60–90 min

0-10

Welcome and framing

Name the Default Test: an underspecified prompt reveals what the model fills in.

10-20

Unplugged prediction

Ask what people picture for "a doctor examining a patient" before seeing any output.

20-45

Pixels and features

Use the feature tool to connect recognition to numeric representation.

45-70

Diffusion steps

Move through denoising sequences and pause on the Default Test example.

70-85

Image Default Test Board

Record prompts, predictions, outputs, assumptions, and revisions.

85-90

Share one claim

Move from verdicts to evidence: what specifically appeared, and under what prompt?

E.

Discussion prompts

What did the model assume that the prompt never specified?
Which visual cue survived longest as resolution dropped?
At what diffusion step did the subject become recognizable?
Does prompting fix a default, or only work around it?
Who benefits from the default image? Who is excluded?
What evidence would make your claim stronger?
