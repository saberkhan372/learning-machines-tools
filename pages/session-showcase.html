Optional studio

Make, Teach, Investigate, Explain, or Critique

What did we learn about generative AI by making with it?

Participants can share what they built, found, tested, adapted, explained, or critiqued. The goal is not impressiveness; it is evidence, reflection, and transfer back to practice.

A.

What we make visible

01

Evidence

Every project points to a prompt, artifact, comparison, or observation.

02

Human judgment

Participants name what they chose, revised, rejected, or refused.

03

System assumptions

Projects connect output behavior to defaults, data, interfaces, and access.

04

Transfer

The cohort leaves with a protocol they can use beyond the camp.

D.

Run of show · 60–90 min

0-10

Welcome and norms

Frame the showcase around evidence, not polish.

10-75

Presentations

Each participant shares for 8-10 minutes, then the group asks what they noticed and what question opened.

75-85

Collective reflection

Name one thing to take back and one question still held.

85-90

Close and consent

Confirm recap permissions, credit preferences, and follow-up interest.

E.

Response prompts

What did you notice?
What question does this open?
What did the human decide?
What is something the machine cannot do that matters?
What evidence did the presenter point to — a specific prompt, output, or comparison?
What did the human decide that the machine couldn't?
What would you need to change to adapt this for your own students or context?
What question are you leaving with that you didn't arrive with?
